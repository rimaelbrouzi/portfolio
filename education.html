Student Researcher at AMHR Lab

As a Student Researcher at the AMHR Lab, I focused on identifying cognitive traits using advanced large language models. My research centered around the Chain Free Association Task, where I explored how individuals’ next-word predictions could reveal underlying cognitive patterns and differences. I was responsible for analyzing extensive datasets, developing Python modules to fine-tune models for enhanced accuracy, and contributing to the understanding of language processing in relation to cognition. I collaborated with senior researchers, leveraged cutting-edge AI technologies, and played a pivotal role in advancing the lab’s goal of using language models to bridge the gap between AI and human cognitive research. My work directly impacted the lab's ability to make significant strides in understanding individual cognitive differences and their relationship with language prediction tasks.
